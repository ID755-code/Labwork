Database Normalization

Purposes of Normalization

  • Data Redundancy: Reduces duplication of data, saving storage space and avoiding inconsistencies.
  • Data Integrity: Ensures accurate data by preventing anomalies in database operations.
  • Efficient Queries: Organizes data to improve query performance and reduce errors.

Normal Forms

First Normal Form (1NF):

Ensures that each column contains only atomic (indivisible) values. No repeating groups or arrays.

Second Normal Form (2NF):

Builds on 1NF by eliminating partial dependencies. All non-key attributes must depend entirely on the primary key.

Third Normal Form (3NF):

Further reduces redundancy by removing transitive dependencies. Non-key attributes depend only on the primary key.

Additional Considerations

  • Boyce-Codd Normal Form (BCNF): A stronger version of 3NF that addresses certain anomalies not handled by 3NF.
  • Fourth Normal Form (4NF) and Beyond: Deal with multi-valued dependencies and further reduce redundancy.

Normalization is essential for creating efficient and scalable databases. However, over-normalization can sometimes lead to performance issues due to excessive table joins. Balancing normalization with practical performance considerations is crucial.
